164 def isocalendar():
165 """Return a 3-tuple, (ISO year, ISO week number, ISO weekday).
166  
167 The ISO calendar is a widely used variant of the Gregorian calendar.
168 See http://www.phys.uu.nl/~vgent/calendar/isocalendar.htm for a good
169 explanation.
170  
171 The ISO year consists of 52 or 53 full weeks, and where a week starts
172 on a Monday and ends on a Sunday. The first week of an ISO year is the
173 first (Gregorian) calendar week of a year containing a Thursday. This
174 is called week number 1, and the ISO year of that Thursday is the same
175 as its Gregorian year.
176  
177 For example, 2004 begins on a Thursday, so the first week of ISO year
178 2004 begins on Monday, 29 Dec 2003 and ends on Sunday, 4 Jan 2004, so
179 that ``date(2003, 12, 29).isocalendar() == (2004, 1, 1)`` and
180 ``date(2004, 1, 4).isocalendar() == (2004, 1, 7)``.
181 """

257 def fromtimestamp(timestamp, tz=None):
258 """Return the local date and time corresponding to the POSIX timestamp.
259  
260 Same as is returned by time.time(). If optional argument tz is None or
261 not specified, the timestamp is converted to the platform's local date
262 and time, and the returned datetime object is naive.
263  
264 Else tz must be an instance of a class tzinfo subclass, and the
265 timestamp is converted to tz's time zone. In this case the result is
266 equivalent to
267 ``tz.fromutc(datetime.utcfromtimestamp(timestamp).replace(tzinfo=tz))``.
268  
269 fromtimestamp() may raise `ValueError`, if the timestamp is out of the
270 range of values supported by the platform C localtime() or gmtime()
271 functions. It's common for this to be restricted to years in 1970
272 through 2038. Note that on non-POSIX systems that include leap seconds
273 in their notion of a timestamp, leap seconds are ignored by
274 fromtimestamp(), and then it's possible to have two timestamps
275 differing by a second that yield identical datetime objects.
276  
277 .. seealso:: `utcfromtimestamp`.

366 dst() methods must not return None. self must be aware (self.tzinfo
367 must not be None, and self.utcoffset() must not return None).
368

370 adjustment of date or time members is performed. Else the result is
371 local time in time zone tz, representing the same UTC time as self:
372

  • E501 Line too long (87 > 79 characters)

377 Time transition boundaries where this cannot be achieved (an issue only
378 if tz models both standard and daylight time).
379  
380 If you merely want to attach a time zone object *tz* to a datetime *dt*
  • E501 Line too long (83 > 79 characters)
